Gray Wolf

Wolves develop close relationships and strong social bonds. They often demonstrate deep affection for their family and may even sacrifice themselves to protect the family unit.

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus
  • Average Length: 1.6 m
  • Average Lifespan: 5-6 years
  • Habitat: Deserts, Grasslands, Forests and Arctic Tundras.

Gray wolves range in color from grizzled gray or black to all-white. As the ancestor of the domestic dog, the gray wolf resembles German shepherds or malamutes. Though they once nearly disappeared from the lower 48 United States, today wolves have returned to the Great Lakes, northern Rockies and Southwestern United States.
